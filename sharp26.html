SHARP 2026 Biomedical Engineering Internship

June 15, 2026 – July 24, 2026 (6 weeks)

Program Contacts

  • Principal Investigator (PI): Pranav Bhounsule (pranav@uic.edu)
  • Teaching Assistant: Nathan Morabito (nmorab2@uic.edu)

Overall Information

  • All projects are listed here: SHARP_2026.pdf
  • Students should obtain a laptop for programming, documentation, analysis, and presentation work. Students should also maintain a dedicated lab notebook for notes, observations, experimental procedures, and results.
  • Students will present their progress weekly using the following presentation template: Weekly Presentation Template
  • The suggested timeline consists of approximately three weeks of laboratory training, literature review, and experimental study development followed by three weeks of experimentation, data collection, analysis, and report preparation.

Expectations

  • Research takes time and patience. Students should maintain an open-minded attitude and understand that failure is a normal part of research.
  • Dead ends and unsuccessful experiments are common and often help guide future ideas and approaches.
  • Research is often unstructured and open-ended. Students should be prepared to adapt ideas and revise hypotheses.
  • Taking breaks and discussing ideas with peers often helps generate new insights and creative solutions.
  • This experience is intended to develop research, communication, presentation, and technical skills.

Week 1

  • Lab 1: Introduction to Arduino in a Simulator (TinkerCAD)

    This lab can be done at home and requires only a computer and a good internet connection.
  • Lab 2: Human Subject Research and Institutional Review Boards (IRBs)

    This lab can be done at home and requires only a computer and a good internet connection.
  • Handout 1: Initial Literature Review

    The literature review can be done at home. However, for some papers, you might need a UIC NetID to access the full text.
  • Presentation of your work to the PI, TA, and peers. Weekly Presentation Template

Week 2

  • Lab 3: Introduction to Arduino in Hardware

    In this lab, you will learn how to set up and use an Arduino microcontroller. This lab will be conducted in person at the UIC campus.
  • Handout 2: Extensive Literature Review

    The literature review can be done at home. However, for some papers, you might need a UIC NetID to access the full text. This literature review will help you create an experimental study for the next week.
  • Presentation of your work to the PI, TA, and peers. Weekly Presentation Template

Week 3

  • Lab 4: Introduction to Sensors for Biomedical Research on Human Subjects
  • Handout 3: Experimental Study Development

    You will develop an experimental study based on the literature review and the sensors you have learned about.
  • Complete the following form and email it to the PI: Study_2026.docx

    NOTE: The deliverable for July 24, 2026 is a report and presentation on the experimental study you developed and conducted.
  • Presentation of your work to the PI, TA, and peers. Weekly Presentation Template

Week 4

  • Instrumentation setup
  • Initial data collection
  • Preliminary data analysis
  • Presentation of your work to the PI, TA, and peers. Weekly Presentation Template

Week 5

  • Continued experimentation
  • Additional data collection
  • Data analysis and interpretation
  • Presentation of your work to the PI, TA, and peers. Weekly Presentation Template

Week 6

  • Final data collection and analysis
  • Final presentation and abstract preparation
  • Final presentation at the July 24, 2026 showcase
