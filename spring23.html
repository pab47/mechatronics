About: A first course in mechatronics. The course deals mainly with electronics: resistors, capacitors, diodes, transistors, digital circuits (logic gates, flip-flops, timers), data acquisition, sensors, and actuators, and a very little design (through a lab and a final project). The course involves theory (see YouTube videos/notes) that is supplemented by TinkerCAD-based labs (a free circuit simulator and 3D design tool) and hardware labs and a hands-on project on creating an interactive animatronics.

If you are an instructor who is interested in this course you might benefit from the following:

  • Textbook: The course content is taken from Intro. to Mechatronics and Measurement by David Alciatore. The book offers a rich collection of homework problems which were also used as assignment (please email me, pranav AT uic dot edu, if you want hw problems)
  • Labs:The lab content (which is hard to find for such a course) was created by me using the course content. If you follow the curriculum below, the labs occur after the necessary course content/hw is covered, which helps the learning outcomes. A detailed breakdown of costs for the labs will be updated soon
  • Project: A final project on creating an animatronics combines skills in design and electronics. The project is fairly open-ended, but requires students to put many skills that they learnt in the course to test.
